Player Philosophy

Technical. Adventurous. Passionate.

T
Technical

The development of technique is critical for Sporting's philosophy. Younger ages before 14 will be heavily focused on individual technical ability. Players will be expected to take on a desire to improve their own technical ability to grow within the club. Coaches will put a large emphasis on technique, introducing players to all playing surfaces — ball control, space, backdrops, and more.

A
Adventurous

"Willing to take risks or try out new methods, ideas, or experiences." As a club we praise those willing to take chances and be creative. Playing within a box or what is expected is not always the answer for a modern player. We encourage Sporting players to play outside the realms of expectations — and to play freely within their own creative ideas. This means Sporting players must have character and personality.

P
Passionate

It's the intent and agreement that each Sporting player must demonstrate passion for the club and their own personal development and growth in the game. Each player should not only have the desire to be at every training session and game, but also have a passionate mindset to work tirelessly for themselves, their team, and their coach. Passion drives players to play with speed, thought, and speed of play.

Coaching Philosophy

How Our Coaches Are Expected to Lead

01
Outcome

Winning and losing is a massive part of the game — nobody sets out to lose. But at youth level it's not the matter of life and death. Coaches will be more concerned on the outcome of development of the team and the individual development to reach short and long term goals. As athletes get older, outcomes of games will more closely reflect the first team.

02
Curriculum

Directors have developed a curriculum with focal points within all age groups. The curriculum is separated by U7–U12, U13–U15, and U16–U18. Coaches will focus their training sessions and game-day experiences to the curriculum in place for the club. We believe this curriculum will help develop our athletes into quality soccer players.

03
Education

Every coach involved with the vision of Sporting Football Club is dedicated to their own growth as a coach leader. Coaching education is a long process, but it is valued by each coach at Sporting to better themselves and everyone involved. With continued education, each coach brings back more structure and development to the players at Sporting.

04
Standards

Each person directly involved with Sporting will be held to the highest standard. All coaches are proud to represent Sporting Football Club and will treat all those involved with the club with respect. Behavior on the sidelines during games and training sessions will be expected to be done with class and in a manner that meets the expectations of Sporting as a whole.

The Sporting Standard

What We Expect From Everyone

Respect — Always
Every player, coach, parent, and official deserves to be treated with respect. On the pitch, on the sidelines, in the parking lot. The Sporting standard doesn't turn off after the final whistle.
Show Up Ready
Every training session and every game is an opportunity. Players are expected to arrive on time, warmed up mentally, and ready to work. Effort is non-negotiable.
Represent the Badge
When you wear the Sporting FC crest you represent every player, coach, and family in this club. Carry it with pride and act in a manner that honors that responsibility.
Grow Every Day
The goal isn't perfection — it's progress. We ask every player and coach to leave every session better than they arrived. That mindset, compounded over years, is what builds champions.

Ready to Be Part of This?

Our philosophy isn't just words on a page — it's how we train, how we compete, and how we carry ourselves every day.
